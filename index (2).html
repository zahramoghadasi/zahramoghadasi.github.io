A life in chapters

My Story

Tehran
1990 – 2023

Growing Up in Tehran

I grew up in a country full of talent and limitations at the same time.

Very early on, I understood that if I wanted a certain life, I would have to build it myself. When things didn't go as planned, I learned to try again, and no matter how many times I fell, to stand up.

That mindset shaped me.

Becoming a Leader in Iran's Tech Space

I started my career in Iran's growing tech ecosystem. Beginning in a telecom firm, I became the youngest female leader during a period of major transformation. I pushed for data-driven marketing and helped the company grow during a critical shift.

Joining the Uber-equivalent in Iran, I moved from analyst to senior manager. I led growth initiatives, built user-focused systems utilising ML, and helped double orders in six months. Later, I shaped the company's IPO — the first startup listing in the country.

Before leaving Iran, I started my own consulting practice, helping businesses in finance, retail, and beauty grow sustainably.

Tehran taught me how to build under pressure, and how to lead without compromising on integrity.

2023
London
2023 – Present

Taking the Leap of Faith

At the peak of my career in Tehran, I moved to London to pursue my MBA at London Business School.

I wanted exposure to different markets, leadership styles, and ways of thinking. LBS offered all of that — friends from over 60 nationalities, an exchange term at Kellogg in the US, a course in Beijing, and activities ranging from leading the Geopolitics Club to AI initiatives and ESG projects expanded the experience far beyond a master's degree.

But I have to admit: starting again in a new country DOES stretch you. Experiencing the Dunning–Kruger effect in real life is far more humbling than reading about it!

Building on the Past

In London, I've worked across different sectors. From unlocking transformation savings in consulting to shaping go-to-market strategy in healthtech, I've been learning how to operate at a different scale.

And I am still building...
